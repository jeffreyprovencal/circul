The Problem

460 million tonnes per year.
9% recycled.

Africa’s informal recycling workers handle a significant share of the world’s collected plastics. The material exists. The workers exist. The buyers exist. The data doesn’t.

🇬🇭

840,000 tonnes. 9.5% collected. Less actually recycled.

Ghana alone generates 840,000 tonnes of plastic waste every year. Only 9.5% is collected for recycling — and much of that never reaches a processor, lost to contamination and missing infrastructure at every handoff. The material exists. The system to move it doesn’t.

Source: Ghana Audit Service · WEF
👐

15–20 million people. No receipts. No records.

Between 15 and 20 million people worldwide survive by collecting recyclable materials — most earning under $2 a day with no written record of what they collected, what it weighed, or what they were paid. Disputes are settled by whoever owns the scale.

Source: WIEGO · World Bank
🇪🇺

Europe needs verified supply. It doesn’t exist yet.

EU regulations mandate 30–35% recycled content in packaging by 2030. Global demand for recycled plastics is projected to exceed supply by 35 million tonnes. The material is sitting uncollected in West Africa. The barrier isn’t volume — it’s the absence of chain-of-custody data that meets EU standards.

Source: PPWR · McKinsey · Interzero
How it works

From street to factory,
one connected chain.

Four tiers. One platform. Every handoff recorded.

01 Collector Free

Collector brings material

Ama walks to the nearest aggregator point with her haul. She logs in with her phone number and a 4-digit PIN — no app, no smartphone needed. She sells her HDPE. The weight, material type, price, and timestamp are recorded on both sides.

📱 Phone + PIN login ⚖ Weight recorded at point of sale 🧾 Receipt in transaction history
02 Aggregator Free

Aggregator grades & weighs

Kwesi weighs the material on his side — independently of Ama’s weight. Both readings are stored. He grades it, confirms the purchase, and Ama’s payment record updates instantly. His inventory grows. No paper. No disputes about who weighed what.

⚖ Dual-weight — both sides record 📦 Inventory builds automatically
03 Processor $39/mo

Processor buys in bulk

rePATRN posts what they want — material type, volume, price. Aggregators in the network sell to them. Every incoming load is logged: weight, origin, grade, supplier. When a batch is ready, rePATRN generates a traceability certificate that traces every kilogram back to its source.

📋 Full supplier network dashboard 📄 Traceability certificate per batch 🛡 EPR & CSRD reports generated
04 Converter $99/mo

Converter closes the loop

Poly Recycling buys verified, certified feedstock. They can trace every kilogram back to the collector who brought it in — street, aggregator, batch, processor. That chain-of-custody data satisfies EU buyers, meets PPWR requirements, and is ready for CSRD reporting. The loop is closed.

🔗 Full chain-of-custody to collector level 🇪🇺 PPWR & CSRD ready 📦 QR product journey — batch to shelf

For processors

You know what you need.
Your suppliers don’t have the data.

Every recycling processor faces the same three problems. Circul solves all three.

Problem 01

You can’t see your supply chain

Your material comes from dozens of aggregators, each with their own records — or no records at all. You have no visibility into who’s selling, what grade, or whether the weight is accurate until it arrives at your gate.

Live supplier network dashboard — every aggregator, every load, in one view
Problem 02

Weight fraud is costing you money

When only one side weighs the material, disputes happen — and you lose. Inflated weights, grading disagreements, and missing receipts erode your margins on every transaction.

Dual-weight system — aggregator and processor weights stored independently on every transaction
Problem 03

Your buyers need proof you can’t provide

EU buyers, EPR schemes, and CSRD-reporting corporates are asking for chain-of-custody documentation. Right now you can’t produce it. That’s locking you out of the premium market.

Traceability certificates generated per batch — traces every kilogram back to its collector
